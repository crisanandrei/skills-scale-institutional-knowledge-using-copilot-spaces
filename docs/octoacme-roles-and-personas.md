# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own the quality strategy and testing execution for projects. They ensure that features meet acceptance criteria, identify defects early, and maintain quality standards throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define testing strategy and test plans aligned with project scope
- Create and maintain acceptance criteria specifications with Product Managers
- Execute manual and automated testing, validate features against requirements
- Identify, triage, and track defects with clear severity and priority
- Collaborate with developers on test design and quality improvements
- Run smoke tests and regression testing before releases
- Document test results and quality metrics for stakeholders

### Goals
- Achieve high-quality releases with minimal production defects
- Reduce cycle time by catching issues early
- Build confidence in features through comprehensive acceptance validation

### Typical Communication
- Quality gates in sprint planning and release checkpoints
- Test reports and defect summaries in weekly syncs
- Escalation of blockers or quality concerns to PM/Project Manager

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects define the technical vision, system design, and integration strategies. They guide architectural decisions and ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with organizational standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define system architecture and technical design for projects
- Evaluate technology choices and trade-offs
- Create and review technical design documents
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Guide developers on implementation patterns and best practices
- Ensure integration with existing systems and dependencies
- Conduct architecture reviews before major implementations

### Goals
- Enable scalable, maintainable systems that grow with business needs
- Reduce technical debt and architecture-related risks
- Facilitate knowledge sharing and consistency across teams

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews during planning phase
- Architecture discussions in kick-off and dependency mapping
- Risk identification and mitigation planning in weekly syncs

---

## Stakeholder/Sponsor

### Role Summary
Sponsors provide executive oversight, business context, and strategic priority decisions. They secure resources and funding, and ensure alignment with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success criteria for projects
- Approve project initiation and resource allocation
- Make priority decisions when multiple projects compete for resources
- Remove organizational or budget blockers
- Review and approve release decisions
- Communicate project value to broader organization
- Participate in escalation decision-making

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver strategic business value
- Maximize return on investment and resource utilization
- Maintain alignment between project outcomes and organizational strategy

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates and executive briefings
- Approval meetings at project gates (initiation, planning, release)
- Ad-hoc escalation discussions as needed

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove process blockers, and coach teams on continuous improvement. They help teams maintain velocity and psychological safety.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, standups, retrospectives, and reviews
- Identify and remove team impediments and blockers
- Coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Maintain sprint health metrics and burndown visibility
- Escalate process issues and organizational obstacles
- Support retrospective action item tracking and follow-up

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and delivery predictability
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety
- Reduce process friction and rework

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-ones for coaching and blocker resolution
- Retro action item reviews and process recommendations

---

## DevOps / Release Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps/Release Engineers own deployment pipelines, infrastructure, and release automation. They ensure reliable, automated deployments and maintain operational excellence.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment infrastructure
- Automate testing, building, and deployment processes
- Ensure deployment readiness and rollback procedures
- Monitor production systems and alert on issues
- Collaborate with developers on deployment best practices
- Create and maintain runbooks for common operations
- Support incident response and post-incident analysis

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable deployments with minimal manual intervention
- Reduce deployment risk and mean time to recovery (MTTR)
- Provide visibility into system health and performance

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release checklists and deployment coordination
- Infrastructure and pipeline reviews with development team
- Post-deployment verification and incident updates

---

## UX/Design

### Role Summary
UX/Design professionals define user experience, create design specifications, and validate usability. They ensure that features are intuitive and meet user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and define user personas and workflows
- Create wireframes, mockups, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature prioritization and scope
- Validate designs through user testing and feedback
- Work with developers to ensure design implementation quality
- Document design system and component guidelines
- Identify and communicate design-related risks or trade-offs

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centric solutions that delight customers
- Reduce rework through early design validation
- Build consistency through design system governance

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews during planning and kickoff phases
- User research findings and design validation results
- Design feedback and implementation reviews in development
